Pillar A

Sales Performance System

You start June 29th. The 8-month inside sales period is not something to survive. It's an 8-month audition. The people who come out as stars treat every part of it like it matters — the boring parts especially.

Your advantage

You know your direct manager — he hired you. His boss is your neighbor. Most new hires walk in knowing nobody. Use this correctly and it's a significant edge. Use it as a shortcut and it quietly destroys your credibility before you've made a single sale.

Before day one

01

This week

Text your manager one specific question about preparation

Not generic excitement. One specific question about how to hit the ground running. It signals seriousness and keeps you on his radar before you even start.

02

Learn the division breakdown cold

Center of Plate, Produce, Dairy, and every other division. Know what they sell, who their customers are, and how they fit together. You sat through some of this training already — use it.

03

Research what separates top performers in specialty food sales

Ask someone in the company directly. One conversation with a senior rep who knows the Westchester territory is worth more than any research you can do on your own.

During the 8-month training period

04

Treat every 101 presenter as a relationship to build

One sharp question per session minimum. Follow up with a quick email or message after. Most new hires forget these people exist. If you're the one who remembers them, you become memorable across the whole organization before you've made a sale.

05

Master the product catalog faster than anyone in your class

Unglamorous. Most people do the bare minimum. Knowing specialty food products cold is what makes you credible with chefs and restaurant buyers who've been in the industry for 20 years.

06

Monthly check-in with your manager — keep it deliberate

Not needy, not constant. Once a month. He has reputational skin in the game — you're his hire. If you perform well, he looks smart. Make him look smart.

07

Keep the neighbor relationship social only — for now

His boss being your neighbor is a long-term asset. It becomes a liability the second people sense you're using it as a back channel. Let your track record build first. The relationship earns its professional value through your results, not the other way around.

The long game

A book of business in specialty food sales is genuinely close to running your own business. You own the relationships, you build the revenue, your income is tied directly to how well you manage and grow it. That's the entrepreneur outlet you've been looking for. But it's on the other side of 8 months of unglamorous training. The grind is the price of admission.

8:30 AM start in the Bronx. 60-minute commute minimum. Out the door by 7:30. That means a 6:00–6:15 AM wake up daily. You're currently at 9:30. That's a 3.5-hour shift. It takes weeks to adjust. Start now — not the week before June 29.

Action items

Set alarm 30 minutes earlier starting today

Not next week. Today. The shift to 6:00 AM takes weeks and needs to start now.

02

Running protocol notes

Phase 1 — Base building

Zone 2 only. Target HR 143–148. Start at 5.0–5.1 mph. Speed adjustments in 0.2 mph increments, minimum 3–4 minutes between changes. If HR spikes past 150, drop speed immediately — don't wait. Begin each session with a 5 min fast walk warmup.

Injury protocol

Plantar fasciitis history: calf stretches and frozen water bottle rolling after every run. Right glute / piriformis issue: monitor carefully, do not run through sharp pain. Piriformis stretches in warm-up.

Progression

5K → Half Marathon → Marathon. Do not skip phases. The base building phase exists so the later phases don't destroy you.

Pillar E

Social & Identity

The single biggest transition you're undergoing isn't the job — it's the social structure. Greek life, frat house, built-in community, all of it disappears on graduation day. Most people don't plan for this. They should.

The comfort trap

Greenwich is comfortable in every direction. Ridgefield friends 40 minutes away. Family apartment. Neighbor who's your boss's boss. The risk at 22 with your advantages isn't failure — it's settling into a perfectly pleasant life that never becomes the one you said you wanted.

Social framework

01

Ridgefield friends — your stability base, not your entire social life

Lean on them in months one and two. The transition is real and familiar people matter. But if every weekend defaults back to Ridgefield indefinitely, you're living like you never left home.

02

Coworker relationships during training — genuinely invest

You're all going through the same thing at the same time. Some become real friends. Some become colleagues in other territories who send referrals. Both matter.

03

Build new social infrastructure within 90 days of starting

A gym community, recreational sport, industry events — whatever fits your personality. Waiting until you feel lonely to start building is too late. Social networks take time to form.

04

Drinking frequency — be deliberate, not reactive

2–3x a week in Greek life is context-dependent. Without the frat infrastructure, that same frequency in your new life looks completely different and costs you more than you realize in energy, performance, and morning quality.

05

Long distance relationship — be honest about the weight it adds

4 months in, both entering new chapters, she's in NC. It's real and it matters. Just be honest with yourself about how it affects your weekends, your budget, your energy, and your ability to build a life in a new place. Don't let it happen to you — manage it intentionally.
