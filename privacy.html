Life OS — Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 1, 2026

Life OS is a private, single-user personal dashboard built and used by one individual. It is not a commercial product and has no other users.

What data is accessed

With the user's explicit authorization, Life OS connects to third-party services (such as WHOOP, Hevy, and similar fitness platforms) to retrieve the user's own health and fitness data — for example recovery, sleep, strain, and workout history. Data is only ever read for the account of the person who authorized the connection.

How data is used

Retrieved data is used solely to display the user's own metrics back to them within the Life OS dashboard. It is not sold, shared, rented, or disclosed to any third party, and it is not used for advertising or analytics.

How data is stored

Data is stored in the user's own private Supabase project, protected by authentication and row-level security. Access tokens for connected services are stored server-side and are never exposed to the browser. The user may delete this data at any time by removing it from their Supabase project.

Revoking access

The user can revoke Life OS's access to any connected service at any time through that service's own account settings (for example, the WHOOP app or developer dashboard). Doing so immediately stops any further data retrieval.

Contact

This application is operated by its sole user. For any questions about this policy, contact the operator at the email associated with the connected accounts.
